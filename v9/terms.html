2. Natural Fabrics and Delicate Materials

Furniture, mattresses, rugs, and upholstery made with natural or delicate fibers, including cotton, linen, viscose, wool, silk, velvet, bouclé, and fabric blends, are among the most challenging materials to clean.

Because these fabrics are highly absorbent and sensitive, strong cleaning solutions cannot be used. As a result, some stains, discoloration, body oils, and heavy soiling — especially those that have been present for several months or years — may not be removable.

Natural fabrics may absorb dyes from clothing, bedding, and everyday use. Prolonged exposure to sunlight may also cause fading, yellowing, or discoloration that cannot be corrected through professional cleaning.

Curly Cleaner LLC will always use the safest cleaning method available for the material, but results on delicate and natural fabrics may be limited.

Previous attempts at cleaning using home products, rental machines, spot removers, or services performed by another company may cause permanent streaking, discoloration, shrinkage, texture changes, or other damage. Curly Cleaner LLC is not responsible for pre-existing conditions or damage caused by previous cleaning attempts.

Professional cleaning is intended to improve the condition and appearance of the item, but complete stain removal or restoration cannot be guaranteed.

4. Drying Time

Several factors affect drying time, including the type of fabric, level of soiling, indoor humidity, temperature, air circulation, and fiber density.

In most cases, upholstery and mattresses require approximately 12–24 hours to dry. Heavily soiled items, thick cushions, and certain fabrics may require additional drying time and, in some cases, up to 48 hours.

Curly Cleaner LLC cannot guarantee an exact drying time, as drying conditions vary from home to home.

Customers are encouraged to use fans, air conditioning, ceiling fans, or open windows (weather permitting) to help speed up the drying process.

Curly Cleaner LLC offers an optional Turbo Drying Service for an additional fee of $50. This service may help accelerate the drying process but does not guarantee a specific drying time.

10. Rug Cleaning On-Site

Curly Cleaner LLC provides on-site rug cleaning services; however, Customers should understand that this is not a full immersion deep-cleaning process. We clean only the top surface of the rug because we are able to access only one side during an on-site appointment.

Rugs that are heavily soiled, contain severe staining, or require soaking and multiple wash cycles may not be suitable for on-site cleaning.

The final determination regarding whether a rug is suitable for on-site cleaning will be made after a physical inspection of the rug.

If furniture is located on the rug, Curly Cleaner LLC will clean only accessible areas. We do not move furniture or clean underneath furniture, except for lightweight chairs or small tables that can be easily moved.

Customers requesting complete rug cleaning are responsible for removing all furniture and items from the rug before the appointment.

After cleaning, rugs must be allowed to dry completely. Customers should not walk on the rug or place furniture back on it until the rug has fully dried.

Some rugs may temporarily distort after wet cleaning. Properly manufactured rugs typically return to their original shape within several days. If the rug fails to flatten after seven days, this is considered a manufacturing defect and is not the responsibility of Curly Cleaner LLC.

Tufted Rugs

Tufted rugs contain latex backing. During cleaning, moisture may weaken the adhesive, resulting in delamination or separation of the backing. Curly Cleaner LLC uses the safest cleaning methods available but is not responsible for the quality of the latex adhesive or the manufacturing process. Delamination is considered a manufacturing defect.

Curly Cleaner LLC exercises great care when cleaning natural fiber rugs. Due to the delicate nature of these materials, Customers acknowledge and understand that:

  1. Natural rugs may shed fibers during and after cleaning. This is a natural characteristic and not considered damage.

  3. Pre-existing stains, wear, and damage may not be fully removable and may become more noticeable after cleaning.
  4. Natural fibers may experience slight texture changes, particularly with handwoven rugs.
